Family Painting

Acrylic Paint and Paper on 6 x 20 Inch Canvas

This piece belongs to a set of two. The two pieces have intertwining meanings that emphasize eachother. Unfortunately, I can't display the other half of the set on this website at the moment. Due to complications of the pandemic, I don't have access to the other piece.

As this was a school project, I was required to have printed images on the canvas. I wanted to have the impression of the photos imitating memories so I opted to have pictures of family. I used photoshop to adjust the hue and saturations of various photos and then stuck them onto the canvas. I then painted the background colours that would blend with the photos.

I wanted the piece to make an impact of emotions not just through the shapes and forms, but also through the colours and gestures made by my brush.

The colours chosen were meant to make you feel somber, but with hints of happiness at the same time. That is why I chose dark blues and purples. The small strokes of pink across the canvas offsets the gloominess and adds the "happiness".

The subjects portrayed in the painting is my brother, and my cousin. I felt that they looked awkward floating in the middle of the canvas so in the highlights and shadows, I added pinks and blues. This brought the piece together in the end.
